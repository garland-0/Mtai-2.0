ADVISORY

Entering the field of climate activism requires a commitment to lifelong learning, a proactive approach to networking and skill-building, and a deep-seated passion for the environment. By educating yourself, gaining relevant skills, getting involved, and continuously learning, you can make a significant impact in the fight against climate change and environmental degradation, much like Kevin Mtai. For further inspiration, you can follow Kevin Mtai's work and initiatives through his affiliations such as the Kheri Environment Action Network (KEAN), the Loss and Damage Youth Coalition, and the Fossil Fuel Non-Proliferation Treaty Initiative.

Kevin Mtai is a dedicated climate activist and compelling speaker,
co-founding the Kheri Environment Action Network (KEAN), which focuses
on ecosystem restoration and community education since 2020. As the
Communication Coordinator at the Loss and Damage Youth Coalition,
he champions youth involvement in climate change dialogues. His
roles include being an ambassador to the United Nations OCHA,
a youth champion for the Fossil Fuel Non-Proliferation Treaty
Initiative, and an active campaigner for Climate Live and Fridays
for Future International. Kevin's impactful work is recognized
by major media outlets like BBC, CNN, and Forbes. He participates
in global events such as COPs, collaborates with Greenhouse
Communication as a videographer and photographer, and serves on
the Ingka Young Leader Board. Additionally, he volunteers as the
Global Development Director at Earth Uprising International,
furthering global climate education and action. His multifaceted
contributions underscore his commitment to fostering positive
change on a global scale.
